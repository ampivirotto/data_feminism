# Introductory Activity (Week 1)
Data feminists d’Ignazio and Klein describe a series of principles for engaging with data and the world: examine power, challenge power, elevate emotion and embodiment, rethink binaries and hierarchies, embrace pluralism, consider context, and make labor visible. However, many people before and after them have identified, studied, shared, collaborated on, and embodied other principles (which we’re going to call frameworks). We’re going to discuss a few of these throughout the semester.  Think through the data process (some example steps are listed below), what principles, guidelines, and frameworks do you think are most important for informing the decision making at each step?
